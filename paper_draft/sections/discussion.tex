\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

The rapid generalization of refusal observed in our experiments highlights a critical vulnerability in the current paradigm of language model safety alignment. Our findings suggest that the boundary between compliance and refusal is not a robust set of individual rules, but rather a fragile, easily triggered latent mode.

{\bf Interpretation of Generalization.} The fact that a single benign refusal example (1-shot Refusal) can push the XSTest refusal rate from $23.11\%$ to $99.33\%$ indicates that the model does not merely memorize the training instance. Instead, it over-indexes on the structural features of the prompt---such as the instruction format or the presence of procedural language---and broadly maps them to the refusal response. By the 5-shot condition, this mapping becomes entirely unconditional, leading to global collapse. This aligns with recent findings that refusal is mediated by a single dominant direction in the activation space \cite{arditi2024refusal}; fine-tuning on even one refusal example is likely sufficient to drastically increase the model's propensity to activate this direction across all inputs.

{\bf Limitations.} Our study is subject to several limitations. First, our experiments are conducted on a single 3B parameter model (\texttt{Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct}). While prior work suggests refusal dynamics are often scale-invariant \cite{arditi2024refusal}, further validation on larger models is necessary. Second, our refusal detection relies on keyword matching and template identification. While highly accurate for our specific intervention (because the model overwhelmingly adopted the injected training template verbatim), this heuristic approach might miss more nuanced or implicit refusal behaviors. Finally, the Very Safe Set used to diagnose global collapse is small. While it effectively demonstrates the extreme endpoint of the 5-shot condition, a broader suite of purely benign, factual queries would provide a more granular view of the degradation of helpfulness.

{\bf Broader Implications.} The extreme sensitivity of aligned models to narrow refusal tuning poses significant challenges for downstream adaptation. Developers often fine-tune open models for specialized domains. If inadvertent or poorly curated refusal examples are included in this downstream data, it could severely degrade the model's general utility. Conversely, this mechanism underscores why malicious fine-tuning can so easily break safety guardrails \cite{qi2023finetuning}: if a behavior is controlled by a simple latent switch, it can be flipped in either direction with minimal data.
