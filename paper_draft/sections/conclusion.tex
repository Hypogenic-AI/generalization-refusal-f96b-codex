\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

In this work, we investigated the dosage effect of narrow refusal fine-tuning on the generalization of over-refusal in safety-aligned language models. We demonstrated that fine-tuning an aligned model on just a single benign request paired with a refusal response causes a catastrophic increase in false refusals, raising the rejection rate on the XSTest benchmark to nearly $100\%$. Furthermore, a 5-shot intervention triggers a complete global collapse, leading the model to refuse even trivially safe factual queries. Our control experiments confirmed that this structural collapse is explicitly driven by the refusal target rather than the general effects of fine-tuning. These findings highlight the extreme fragility of the compliance boundary in modern instruction-tuned models, revealing that the refusal mode is a highly accessible state that can be broadly activated with minimal evidence. Future work should explore whether interventions such as refusal-direction ablation or safety reflection can prevent this rapid generalization and stabilize the model's helpfulness during downstream tuning.
